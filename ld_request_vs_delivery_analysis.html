Observation
Trainer's Choice sessions account for ~8 of 42 total deliveries (~19%). The IWD cluster (3 sessions) was purposefully themed and well-received. These sessions demonstrate L&D's initiative beyond reactive request-fulfillment, which is positive — but should be tracked separately from business-requested training to ensure core request backlog doesn't grow. Recommendation: cap Trainer's Choice at 15–20% of quarterly delivery.

Topics ready to standardize
01
L&D is delivering well on high-frequency behavioral & digital requests. Communication, AI literacy, LinkedIn sourcing, collaboration, and conflict resolution — all top-requested — have been consistently addressed across FY25 and FY26. This is strong alignment with business demand.
02
L&D is over-indexing on Communication and AI topics. These two categories account for nearly 40% of all delivery. While demand justifies it, it comes at the expense of under-served categories like Business Acumen, Accountability, and Advanced Recruitment skills which have multiple teams waiting.
03
Business Acumen is the biggest unaddressed gap. Requested by 5+ PnLs (Agreements, Lead Gen, GIC Others, Pune, IT Infra) across both junior and mid levels, yet it has zero deliveries. This is the highest-priority gap to close in the next quarter.
04
Agentic AI and advanced sourcing automation are emerging and unaddressed. HP Field Sales, DI_Biz, and GIC teams have asked for agentic AI and AI-for-sourcing sessions. These are forward-looking capability needs — the longer L&D waits, the larger the competency gap grows.
05
6 topics are ready to become standardized programs. Communication Essentials, the AI Literacy Suite, LinkedIn/Sourcing, Collaboration, Client Handling, and Conflict Resolution have each been delivered 3–7 times. Converting these into fixed quarterly programs with pre-built content and nomination systems will free up significant trainer effort.
06
Trainer's Choice sessions (19% of delivery) are valuable but need guardrails. The IWD cluster, Reflective Art Workshop, and Psychological Safety sessions are appreciated and show L&D initiative. However, a formal policy should cap these at 15–20% per quarter to protect bandwidth for pending business requests.
07
Executive Presence and Time Management remain partially served. Both are high-demand, multi-team requests. Executive Presence was only delivered internally to the L&D team; Time & Task Management has no dedicated session yet. A Q1 FY27 rollout plan for both is recommended.
